About Fire Safety

What Is Fire Risk?

Fire (sunog) is a hazard often underestimated until it’s too late. Whether caused by faulty wiring, cooking accidents, or natural causes, a small flame can turn into a destructive blaze — especially in dense neighborhoods or poorly insured homes.

Why Preparedness Matters:

The Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP) consistently emphasizes fire prevention through public education, fire-safe homes, and rapid response. An escape plan, regular maintenance, and the right tools can save lives when minutes count.

Before (Prevention):

  • Inspect your home’s electrical wiring and appliances for frayed wires, exposed cords, or overloaded outlets.
  • Store flammable materials (e.g., gasoline, paint, paper) safely in ventilated and cool areas.
  • Install smoke alarms where possible and test them monthly.
  • Keep a fire extinguisher accessible, and make sure household members know how to use it.
  • Create and practice two escape routes from every room; designate a meeting point outside.

During (When Fire Happens):

  • If you see smoke, stay low — smoke rises. Crawl along the floor to the nearest exit.
  • Do not use elevators during a fire — always use stairs.
  • If your clothing catches fire: Stop. Drop. Roll. Cover your face, lie down, and roll gently until the flames are out.
  • As soon as it’s safe, call emergency services (see Hotlines page) and alert everyone to evacuate immediately.

After (Recovery):

  • Do not re-enter a burned building until fire authorities declare it safe.
  • Seek medical attention for burns or smoke inhalation.
  • Report fire damage to your barangay or local DRRM office and coordinate with relief agencies for recovery.
  • Review and update your fire escape plan and check your safety equipment regularly.
