About Earthquake

What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake (lindol) is the sudden trembling of the ground caused by shifting tectonic plates. It can trigger ground ruptures, landslides, liquefaction, and tsunamis, especially in the Philippines along the Pacific Ring of Fire.

Why It Matters:

According to PHIVOLCS, collapsing buildings are the greatest risk during strong earthquakes. Quick and correct reactions, along with structural safety, can save lives.

Before (Prepare):

  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls.
  • Practice "Duck, Cover, Hold" under sturdy furniture.
  • Prepare a Go-Bag: water, food, flashlight, medicines, important documents.
  • Plan safe meeting points and evacuation routes.

During (When Shaking):

  1. Duck, cover, and hold — protect your head and neck.
  2. If outside, move to open ground away from buildings and trees.
  3. If driving, stop safely and stay in your vehicle until shaking stops.

After (Recovery):

  • Expect aftershocks; avoid damaged buildings.
  • Check for gas leaks and structural damage.
  • Help injured people if safe and report to local DRRM authorities.
