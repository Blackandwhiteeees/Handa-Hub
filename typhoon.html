About Typhoon & Flood

What Are Typhoon and Flood Risks?

Typhoons (bagyo) bring torrential rain, damaging winds, and sometimes storm surge — a dangerous rise in sea level. Flooding (pagbaha) can result from persistent rain, overflowing rivers, or surge from the sea. These phenomena are monitored by PAGASA and local authorities.

Why It Matters

The Philippines is hit by around 20 typhoons each year. Communities that prepare in advance save more lives. Daily disaster risk management, even before storms arrive, is crucial.

Before (Prepare)

  • Monitor PAGASA bulletins and flood warnings carefully.
  • Prepare a Go-Bag: water, non-perishable food, flashlight, radio, extra clothes, important documents in waterproof containers.
  • Secure windows, reinforce structures, and clear gutters and drainage paths.
  • Identify local evacuation centers and safe routes; practice evacuation plans with your family.

During (When Typhoon or Flood Hits)

  • Stay indoors if safe, away from windows during strong winds.
  • If flooding starts, move to higher floors or ground as instructed by officials.
  • Never drive through floodwaters — even shallow water can sweep vehicles away.
  • If storm surge is forecast, evacuate to higher ground immediately.

After (Recovery)

  • Avoid floodwater — it may be contaminated or electrically charged.
  • Disinfect household items that got wet; ventilate and dry flood-damaged areas.
  • Follow local DRRM and government instructions before returning home.
  • Seek medical attention if exposed to polluted water or if anyone falls ill after the flood.

Storm Surge Specific

Coastal areas face one of the highest risks from storm surge. Know your local surge zones, prepare evacuation plans, and move to high ground when necessary. Plan ahead and act quickly when warnings are issued.
